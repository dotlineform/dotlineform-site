Indeed, even algorithmically generated forms can sometimes be *mimicked* through analog phenomena:

* **Graphite powder dispersion** from pressure and surface texture
* **Wrist-induced micro-jitter** in manual line drawing
* **Paper fiber interaction** with the tip of a pencil
* **Mental heuristics** for repeating pattern-like behaviors

In this context, “impossible to draw” should not merely mean “time-consuming or detailed,” but rather “unrepeatable without computational randomness or precision.”

To truly move toward **non-drawable** designs, I will need to:

* Layer in **non-linear recursive noise** whose variation exceeds perceptual planning
* Introduce **controlled chaos** where patterns subtly mutate in ways the eye can’t anticipate
* Emphasize **density and overlap** at frequencies exceeding traditional mark-making resolutions

This suggests a new target: not simply complexity, but **fundamentally undecidable line behavior** — where no logical manual process can fully reproduce the result, even given infinite time.
