# Memory — Beyond Conscious vs. Subconscious

## Why the duality is too simple

The classic split between “conscious” and “subconscious” is a useful shorthand, but modern cognitive neuroscience favors a **layered, networked architecture** with multiple interacting processes that differ in access, capacity, timescale, and function. Frameworks such as *Global Workspace Theory*, *Predictive Processing*, and *Neurophenomenology* all point to graded, dynamic organization rather than a binary divide.

## Layered view of mind processes

![Layered diagram of mind processes beyond a simple conscious–subconscious split]([[media:docs/library/img/beyond-conscious-subconscious-image-01.png]])

- **Conscious awareness**: reportable, capacity-limited access; often linked to frontoparietal “global broadcasting.”
- **Preconscious / implicit**: content not currently accessed but available to working memory and control.
- **Automatic / procedural**: skills and habits in cortico–basal ganglia–cerebellar loops.
- **Affective / interoceptive**: rapid appraisals and body-state mapping (amygdala, insula).
- **Spontaneous / default mode**: mind-wandering, scene construction, self-projection (DMN).

## Key frameworks

### Global Workspace Theory (GWT)

Information becomes conscious when it is *globally broadcast* to many specialized systems; numerous unconscious processors continue to operate in parallel. Empirical signatures include frontoparietal ignition and long-range synchrony.

### Predictive Processing / Free-Energy Principle (FEP)

The brain is a hierarchical generative model that minimizes prediction error (free energy). Conscious contents reflect high-level model states with integrated precision-weighting rather than a separate “store.”

### Neurophenomenology

Combines first-person reports with neural dynamics; emphasizes graded, context-sensitive consciousness and the coupling of bodily states, affect, and cognition.
